1.3.2. Positional Requirements

Point Guard (PG) The point guard (PG) position typically requires a high level of play-making ability. In the modern game, point guards may be considered either a pass-first or shoot-first guard depending on the individual strengths of the player, the make-up of the team around them, and their teams playing style. Positional Key Metrics: PTS_per_game, AST_per_game, TOV_per_game, AST_to_TOV_ratio, STL_per_game

Shooting Guard (SG) The shooting guard position has always been one for the best shooters in the competition. SGs need to be able to provide their team with points, should have good shooting percentages, particularly 3-point percentage, but also still be able to move the ball and carry an assist to turnover ratio of greater than 1.00. Shooting guards are not traditionally viewed as being active on the defensive end, however, great shooting guards of the past (Michael Jordan and Kobe Bryant) both won defensive player of the year titles during their playing careers. Key Metrics: PTS_per_game, FGM, FGp, 3PM, 3Pp, FTp, AST_per_game, TOV_per_game, AST_to_TOV_ratio, STL_per_game

Small Forward (SF) The small forward position is viewed as a “3 & D” position or a “wing” position in the modern game. Successful small forwards in the modern game must be able to spot up and hit the three from the wing, and play good transition defence. Key Metrics: PTS_per_game, TRB_per_game, AST_per_game, FTp, STL_per_game, BLK_per_game

Power Forward (PF) Modern PFs need to be able to shoot from range, spreads the floor and provide a range of defensive traits. They are typically strongly built, athletic and able to produce on both ends of the court. Key Metrics: Points, Rebounds, FGM, FG%, FT%,

Centre (C) The modern day C needs to be able to run the floor, spread the court with some shooting range and provide a defensive presence. Modern day C’s are almost the typical power forward of old. Positional Key Metrics: Rebounds, FG%, Blocks

5. Player recommendations

Starter Criterion To establish a criterion to determine whether a player was a starting-level calibre of player, the team analysists determined that a player had to have started 70% of games in the 2018-19 NBA Season to be considered a ‘Starter’. This criterion allowed for ‘Non-Starters’ to be filtered out the player database, thus reducing the available players to be selected to the starting five to 140 players from an initial database of 576 players.

The Starting 5

Point Guard: James Harden James Harden is selected to the point guard position as he ranks 1st in Starting PGs in points per game (36.1), and individual efficiency rating (33.09). Harden ranked 18th and 16th in the value metrics ($/efficiency and $/points per game respectively), but despite this, and his hefty price tag (salary: $30,570,000), his points per game (the highest in the league) was irresistible when considering that the team data suggests that the more points a team scores per game, the more likely they are to win. Harden also ranked 1st in steals per game, and 9th in assists per game for additional key metrics at the point guard position.

James Harden was selected ahead of D’Angelo Russell ($7,019,698), Ben Simmons ($6,434,520), Jamal Murray ($3,499,800), Trae Young ($5,363,280).

Shooting Guard: Devin Booker Devin Booker is selected as the shooting guard. Booker presents amazing value whilst still on his rookie contract (\(3,314,365). Booker ranked second on both value metrics (\)/EFF and $/points per game) to Malcolm Brogdon, but he ranked 1st for shooting guards in points per game (26.6) and third for individual player efficiency (23.0) behind Bradley Beal (24.2) and Jrue Holiday (23.0). Devin Booker was the easiest player to recommend, as he ranks in the top 4 in the key metrics, and he remains on a small contract (due to his rookie contract). Devin Booker also ranked 2nd in assists per game, and 5th in free throw percentage for additional key metrics at the shooting guard position.

Devin Booker was selected ahead of Malcolm Brogdon ($1,544,951).

Small Forward: Kawhi Leonard Kawhi Leonard is selected in the small forward position. Fresh from leading his team to the 2018-19 NBA Championship with the Toronto Raptors, Leonard put up 26.6 points per game (ranked 3rd amongst starting small forwards), with an individual player efficiency rating of 26.87 (ranked 4th). Leonard also ranked 2nd in steals per game, 3rd in total rebounds per game and 6th in assists per game for additional key metrics at the small forward position.

Whilst playing the same position as LeBron James, Kevin Durant, and Paul George, Kawhi Leonard was chosen over these players as he had much better value metrics, with his 23.1-million-dollar salary between seven and twelve million dollars cheaper than his rivals. Other notable players at the position were the young trio of Jayson Tatum, Brandon Ingram, and Julius Winslow. Ultimately, these players are unable to match the output on the court that Kawhi has been proven he is able to produce.

Power Forward: Giannis Antetokounmpo Giannis Antetokounmpo was selected as the first choice at the power forward position. Giannis ranked first at the power forward position for points per game (27.7) and for individual player efficiency (35.25). Antetokounmpo’s efficiency score of 35.25 was the highest of all classified ‘Starters’ in the 2018-2019 NBA season. Despite ranking 14th for his position for each of the value metrics, Antetokounmpo still represents good spending at $24,157,304 for the 2019-20 NBA season.

Antetokounmpo also ranked 1st in total rebounds per game and blocks per game, 2nd in assists per game, and 4th in steals per game for additional key metrics at the power forward position.

Giannis Antetokounmpo was selected ahead of Pascal Siakam ($1,544,951) in main part due to his points per game and efficiency ratings being far superior to Siakam’s.

Centre: Karl-Anthony Towns Karl-Anthony Towns is selected at the centre position. Averaging a 24.6 points per game (ranked third at the position), Towns also ranked third in individual efficiency (30.3). Karl-Anthony Towns ranked 8th for his position in $/EFF ($258,000/EFF point), and 7th in $/points per game ($321,000/point per game).

Towns also ranked 5th in total rebounds per game, and 9th in blocks per game for additional key metrics at the centre position.

Karl-Anthony Towns was selected ahead of other value options Jarrett Allen ($2,034,120) and Thomas Bryant ($1,378,242) due to his higher points per game and efficiency rating. Whilst he was also able to match the production of Joel Embiid ($25,467,250) and Anthony Davis ($25,434,263) at a much affordable price (Karl-Anthony Towns - $7,839,435).

7. Limitations

There are some limitations to this statistical analysis. As described above, the suggested starting five would likely exceed an 82-0 win-loss record based off the points per game variable and its correlation to games won in the regular season.

Player data was looked at from a per game perspective. Per minute calculations may be used to establish which players provide statistical impact per minute of playing time. Applying a per-minute approach however may also skew the data in favour to those ‘non-starting’ players that perform well in limited minutes of court time but may not be able to reproduce that output were they playing the higher minutes that ‘starters’ typically play.

Players were additionally sub-categorised as a ‘Starter’ if they had started 70% of games in the 2018-19 season, and as a ‘Non-Starter’ if they had started less than 70% of games in the season. However, having a criterion to meet to be considered a starting five player, provides protection for the data analysist when discussing potential options with the General Manager as you can be assured that the player can play a starting role.
